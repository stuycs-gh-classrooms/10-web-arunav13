Introduction to Stem Cells

Stem cells consist of the cells in our body that will differentiate to form specialized cells. These cells are involved in embryonic growth, regeneration, and repair. They're even leveraged by scientists to create new treatments>

When are Stem Cells at play in our body?

There are many different applications of Stem cells in our body.

  • Embryonic Growth: During embryonic growth, stem cells rapidly proliferate to create the differentiated cells that will eventually become the various tissues and organs of our body.
  • Repair and Damage: If a substantial amount of cells are lost, the body's stem cells will replace the cells that died in that region.
  • Regeneration: In some regenerating organisms, like flatworms and hydras, stem cells are crucial to enable the regeneration of specialized organs.
